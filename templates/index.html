Red wine quality is assessed using physicochemical properties such as acidity, alcohol content, and sulfur compounds, which influence taste and aroma. Quality is typically rated on a scale from 0 to 10, with most wines falling between 3 and 8. Higher-quality wines often show balanced alcohol levels, suitable acidity, and well-controlled sulfite content for preservation. Lower volatile acidity is generally preferred, as it prevents unpleasant vinegar-like flavors. These chemical measurements are widely used to estimate wine quality in data analysis and machine learning models.
