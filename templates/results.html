Quality Rating Scale

In analytical studies, red wine is often classified into three categories based on the sensory scores (0-10).

  • Low Quality: Scores of 3 or 4 indicate wines with significant flaws, such as off-flavors, excessive acidity, or imbalance. These wines may have undesirable characteristics and are generally considered below average.
  • Medium Quality: Scores of 5 or 6 represent wines that are acceptable but may have some minor flaws or lack complexity. These wines are often drinkable and can be enjoyable, but they may not have the depth or balance of higher-quality wines. The majority of wines in the dataset typically fall into this category.
  • High Quality: Scores of 7 or 8 indicate wines that are well-balanced, with good aroma, flavor, and overall quality. These wines are often considered above average and may have desirable characteristics such as complexity, smoothness, and a pleasant finish.
